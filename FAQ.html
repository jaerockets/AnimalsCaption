Animals With Captions FAQ

How do you make your memes/What font do you use?

I use the MediaForge discord bot for a lot of stuff, the white text with black outline in the middle can be done with "$whisper [caption]". The other style of text I frequently use is "$tenor", which can be shown on the bottom by placing a | before the text [if you want text on top and bottom, you just put the | between the two lines of text]. If the caption covers up part of the image, I do the command on a png with all transparent pixels, and then just overlay it on top of the source image with paint.net [on pc] or ibispaint [mobile]. To paste a transparent image, make sure to ctrl+shift+v so it pastes in a new layer, or just create a new layer and post it there [otherwise you just get the grey and white png background stuff]. My go-to fonts for memes made without mediaforge are Lucida Sans Narrow [which I use a lot for the red arrow with black outline memes] and recently I guess comic sans. However, I think it's more fun to find a font you like best- that's how new formats are created! For outlined text in paint.net, you will need to install a plugin. I use this one. Once you add it to pdn, it will show up under the "effects" section. For some memes I also use MS Paint, which is good for shitposts but very light on usability.

Do I need to caption it?

If you want. If you do caption it, I would also appreciate if you sent the source image in case I have my own idea for it.

Did you see my submission?

Yes. I have DMs set to screen for people I don't follow, so it won't show that I read it, but I check submissions daily. I only choose submissions that I both like personally and think fit the overall vibe of the account, so don't feel bad if I didn't use yours. I appreciate each and every submission.

Money. If you don't want to see them, mute the text in the promo tweet, they give you a script so all the promo tweets from every account will be the same for the most part.

Do you have a posting schedule?

Sort of. I mostly post when I'm awake and feel like making memes/checking submissions, which is between the hours of 8 AM and 10 PM Pacific for the most part. My schedule varies from day to day, so there will be some variance here. I also schedule posts in ~30 min intervals if I make a lot at once, because posting them all at once gets less interaction. Scheduling on mobile is more of a pain because I have to do it through the mobile browser site, so I usually just wait ~15 minutes between posts if I'm not at my PC.
